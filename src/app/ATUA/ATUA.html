ATUA

Context

ATUA is a fictional interactive performance project where dancers perform on stage, accompanied by graphic interactions in the background that respond to their movements. Designed as an experience blending dance and technology, this project explored the integration of dynamic visual effects synchronized with the artists, creating an immersive experience for the audience. The audience will also have the opportunity to test the system after the performance.

Art Direction

In this project, I worked on creating visual interactions to enhance the audience's experience. My approach aimed to design an aesthetic and interactive environment in the background of the stage, capable of responding in real time to the dancers' movements. This visual integration needed to support the atmosphere while remaining fluid and captivating. The wave effects symbolized the energies and spirits from Caledonian mythology. The visual impact of ATUA relied on the contrast between the darkness of the stage and the dynamic light effects, highlighting the energy and grace of the dancers. The choice of colors and visual movements aimed to amplify the emotion of the performance while maintaining the fluidity and simplicity of the show.

Interaction Techniques

To design these interactions, I worked with several technologies to choose the most suitable one:

  • TouchDesigner : for creating real-time visual animations.
  • Kinect : for capturing the dancers' movements and transmitting them to the animations.
  • Processing : for prototyping the interactions, exploring effects, and experimenting with different animation styles.
