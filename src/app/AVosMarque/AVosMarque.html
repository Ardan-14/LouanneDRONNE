On your marks

Context

"On your marks" was my final project in the MMI (Multimedia and Internet Professions) program, which I completed as part of a team of six. This multifaceted project put our skills to the test through various tasks: creating two web documentaries, designing a graphic charter, developing an interactive website, organizing a forum, and producing three video games using the Phaser engine. Our ultimate goal was to create a comprehensive communication campaign for the New Caledonian Athletics League, so we reached out to key figures in the field. Here are the specific areas I had the opportunity to work on in more detail:

Art Direction

One of the first steps of the project was creating zoning and wireframes, which was done by students specializing in communication strategy. On a smaller scale, I contributed to the creation of our graphic charter. This charter helped define the visual identity across all our materials, including colors, typography, graphic elements, and icons. The goal was to create aesthetic consistency across all our productions while maintaining the dynamic and modern feel we envisioned for our project.

Web site

Building on the previous work, I then designed the website. This site was conceived as a showcase where users can explore our various work: the web documentaries, video games, and informational content.

Video games

To create a more interactive experience, I developed three video games using the Phaser engine. These games were designed to complement our project by offering an interactive experience that highlights the spirit of our project’s theme. Each game features unique mechanics and is directly tied to the world of athletics, allowing for full immersion in our content.

Web documentarie

We produced two web documentaries. Together with my partner Camille, I created one of them, Les Pistes du Silence, which explores the journey of deaf athletes and sheds light on their experiences in the world of sports. Through impactful editing, I aimed to convey the challenges that this disability presents in the field of athletics.
